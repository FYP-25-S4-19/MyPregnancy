1. Introduction 
1.1 Overview  
The My pregnancy application is a mobile platform designed to support women throughout their 
pregnancy journey by providing free access to health information, nutrition guidance, and 
voluntary consultation with Healthcare Doctors. The application helps to bridge the gap for users 
who are unable to afford paid extra medical services by enabling communication with 
certificated volunteer Doctors.  
Pregnancy is a crucial phase in a woman’s life, it requires special attention to both physical and 
mental health. Women need accurate information during their pre-pregnancy, pregnancy, and 
post-pregnancy periods about nutrition, self-care, and medical guidance to ensure a healthy 
pregnancy. However, many women face challenges in obtaining such support due to limited 
financial resources, lack of access to healthcare professionals, or insufficient awareness of 
available services, which makes many miss essential care for themselves and their babies. 
Recognising these challenges, this project is to develop a mobile application called “My 
Pregnancy”, a free platform that offers educational resources, information, and consultation to 
pregnant women. This app also allows medical doctors to help or give advice. 
This app is designed to be user-friendly for pregnant women and doctors. All features in this app 
can be used freely, but registered users will get additional features, like: 
1. Read articles about pregnancy, nutrition, and baby care 
2. Consult freely with verified Doctors  
3. Share and get information from other users through the Discussion Forum 
4. Receive notifications and reminders for checkups and health tips 
5 

By creating this app, we aim to make pregnancy-related information and basic services more 
accessible to everyone, raise awareness about the importance of care before-during-after 
pregnancy and without financial or geographical barriers. 
We will also provide various convenience features that usually come with such apps to assist in 
helping expectant mothers learn more about their pregnancy such that they can make informed 
decisions, as well as keep a handle on their emotional well-being. 
1.2 Project Scope 
1.2.1 Objectives: 
The My Pregnancy app aims to: 
1. Educate and Empower: 
 Provide verified, easy-to-understand educational resources on pregnancy, nutrition, and 
self-care so that women can make informed decisions during all pregnancy stages. 
2. Promote Health Awareness: 
 Help expectant mothers monitor their well-being by tracking physical and emotional 
indicators such as mood, symptoms, and appetite. 
3. Enhance Accessibility to Support: 
 Connect pregnant women with volunteer Doctors (doctors, nutritionists) for free video or 
messaging consultations, removing financial and geographic barriers. 
4. Facilitate Pregnancy Planning: 
 Provide tools to calculate and track the expected due date and pregnancy milestones 
throughout the journey. 
5. Encourage Community Support: 
 Build an interactive forum that allows mothers to share experiences, seek advice, and 
learn from one another in a safe, supportive environment.

Despite the rise in popularity of various telemedicine and teleconsultation apps, only 2 out of 
the 6 major pregnancy applications we researched offer some form of consultation. 
And even in those apps, consultations are usually offered as a paid feature, preventing an even 
larger portion of the userbase who are un-willing or unable to spend money for such a feature.  
 
Access to timely, expert advice - as important as it is - may be difficult to access for various 
reasons, such as geographic barriers (living far away from a hospital) or financial constraints. 
Users end up relying on generic, non-tailored advice from places like unmoderated community 
forums or word-of-mouth, which can sometimes cause more harm than good. 
 
Our app aims to democratize access to consultation services by making them free for all by 
allowing physicians and obstetricians to volunteer their expertise.  
To maintain quality and trust, our solution ensures a rigorous manual verification process for all 
doctors, ensuring that only those who have properly proved their ownership of the relevant 
license/certification may be allowed to provide birthing-related consultation advice on our 
platform.

1.4. Project Description 
This project aims to develop an user-friendly mobile application, application name My 
Pregnancy, designed to help women through each and every stage of their maternal journey 
before, during and after pregnancy. Understanding that many women might face barriers such 
as limited access to healthcare, education and resources due to the financial obstacles. 
Therefore, this application is providing a free platform offering verified educational resources, 
community-driven support, and volunteer Doctor consultation. 
 
The application promotes both physical and emotional well-being by combining the informative 
article, discussion forums and tracking tools, reminder based on all personalized. By doing this it 
bridges the gap between healthcare knowledge and accessibility, to empower women to 
manage their pregnancy confidently, safely and with the necessary support at every stage of 
their pregnancy even though it might be their first time.

1.5. Business model 
1.5.1. Key Partners 
The key partners in our business model are: 
1. Healthcare Professionals: Volunteer doctors, nutritionists, and Doctors who can provide 
consultation and information. 
2. Merchant to allow pregnant women to purchase items that are required during the post 
pregnancy. 
1.5.2. Key Activities 
Our key activity focuses on development to serve mothers by providing them with accurate 
information, emotional support, and easy access to healthcare professionals. These activities 
are carefully designed to ensure that the app remains reliable, user-friendly, and impactful for 
women throughout their maternal journey. This includes: 
1. App development & maintenance for continuous updates, bug fixes, and UX 
improvements. 
2. Content creation & curation where the research, verification, and updating of articles is 
done on pregnancy, nutrition, and baby care. 
3. Doctor engagement to recruit, verify, and manage every volunteer Doctor. 
4. Community management to ensure user safety, and encouraging engagement. 
5. Public support on technical assistance, FAQ updates, and troubleshooting. 
1.5.3. Key Resources 
The following key resources that our business model has adopted are: 
1. Technological platforms: Cloud-based service providers such as AWS to handle 
secure data storage, user authentication, etc. 
2. API Integrations: Access to 3rd-party APIs to provide things such as  
a. Video calling and real-time messaging for our consultations feature 
b. Verification of medical certificate information against public registries 
 
3. Doctor Network: Collaboration with healthcare professionals such as nutritionists to 
validate nutritional information, provide professional consultations, and develop 
scientifically-backed dietary recommendations. 
 
1.5.4. Value Propositions 
These are a few value propositions that we have proposed with our business model:  
1. Free access to reliable information such as articles, tips, and guidelines for pregnancy 
stages. 
21 

2. Volunteer Doctor consultation where expert guidance without financial barriers. 
3. Community support - discussion forums for sharing experiences and advice. 
4. User-friendly design that leads to easy navigation, notifications, and tracking tools. 
5. Emotional and physical well-being supporting mental health, reminders for checkups, 
and nutrition guidance. 
6. Accessible to users regardless of financial status or location. 
1.5.5. Customer Relationship 
For the My Pregnancy mobile application, the relationship model is semi-self-service, where 
users primarily interact with the platform independently while still having access to support and 
expert guidance when needed. This relationship model is self-service. Users can 
independently explore all the educational resources, such as articles, videos, and participate in 
discussion forums. This app provides personal assistance. Users can receive personalized 
guidance tailored to their individual circumstances. Automated engagement features such as 
notifications and reminders for health checkups, prenatal tips, nutrition advice, and postnatal 
guidance help women stay on track. Finally, the app emphasizes trust-based relationships. 
Verified Doctors, accurate content, and moderated community interactions build credibility and 
reliability.

2. Requirements Motivation 
Pregnancy is a serious and universal human experience, and this is the only process where life 
continues. Yet, this transformative journey often comes with need for loyal support and 
honorable information that can be hard to find. In today’s world, mothers face an enigma: they 
are surrounded by infinite online information, but starved for guidance that is reliable and feels 
personally safe. Sifting through this sea of unverified content can be overwhelming, leaving 
numerous women feeling anxious, confused, and unsafe for their health. 
23 

While there are many pregnancy apps available, they often disappoint. They can feel neutral, 
offering broken tools that lack genuine expert access, deep personalization, or a sense of 
community. We created the "My Pregnancy" app to bridge this exact gap—to build a tool that 
can truly understand and encounter the needs of pregnant women. 
Our core motivation is simple: to help and to fix what is missing. We see the obstacle women 
facing whether it's the cost of healthcare, living far from a clinic, or the simple difficulty of 
knowing what advice to trust. Many existing apps don't really help a lot; they offer a common 
content or focus on general basis over real support. We knew a better solution had to be broad, 
caring for a woman's emotional and knowledgeability health with the same importance as her 
physical health. That’s why "My Pregnancy" is free. 
The motivation for this project is to address these challenges by developing a comprehensive 
pregnancy mobile application that offers: 
1. Trusted Educational Resources: Curated articles, expert videos, and interactive 
materials to help mothers understand every stage of pregnancy, from conception to 
childbirth. 
2. Personalized Health Tracking: A user-friendly interface for tracking key metrics such as 
mood, symptoms, appetite, sleep, and weight, helping users make informed health 
decisions. 
3. Expert Consultation: Access to certified obstetricians and healthcare professionals for 
free video or chat consultations, providing timely medical advice and reassurance. 
4. Community Engagement: A built-in discussion forum where expectant mothers can 
connect, share experiences, and seek peer or expert support, fostering a sense of 
belonging. 
5. Due Date and Progress Monitoring: Smart tools for calculating and tracking expected 
due dates, trimester progress, and important milestones in an easy, visually engaging 
format. 
"My Pregnancy" aims not to just be an app. It can be your dedicated partner. Our mission is to 
provide essential support and knowledge for every mother. Ultimately, we are driven by a 
simple, sincere commitment: to empower you with the confidence, safety, and compassion you 
deserve, ensuring you and your baby have the healthiest, most supported start possible.

3.2 Non-Functional Requirements  
3.2.1 Performance 
Startup Latency: Usability research shows that as page load time increases over 3 seconds, 
the chance that a user bounces away greatly increases 
98 

 
 
As such, we generally will aim to keep our initial app startup time short, ideally under 3 seconds 
for a project of this scope 
 
Real-time Latency: Sluggish performance may also cause a user to become frustrated while 
using the app, and uninstall in favour of our competitors. As such, we aim to keep general 
performance snappy. 
3.2.2. Usability 
Visual and Functional consistency: Our app should, first and foremost, be intuitive and 
user-friendly. Elements should appear to be stylistically and functionally consistent. 
Users should be able to intuitively navigate around the app to find whatever they need. 
A user should easily be able to discover whether or not our app has certain functionality, and 
learn to operate it with relative ease 
 
Responsivenesss: Our app should be able to resize and render correctly on various screen 
sizes 
 
Errors (such as our API returning an error) should be handled gracefully, with clear and concise 
error messages to the users on what went wrong - as well as any corrective course of action. 
3.2.3. Security & Privacy 
Given the sensitive nature of pregnancy (and medical data, as a whole), ensuring that our data 
is secure from unauthorized access and bad actors is arguably one of the most critical 
requirements 
If we indeed choose to deploy to AWS infrastructure, then our best bet would be to leverage 
their vendor-specific methods of ensuring that their data is kept encrypted both in-transit and 
at-rest. 
99 

3.2.4. Reliability & Availability 
Uptime: The app should have minimal down-time, such that users can access the app at almost 
all times. Regular outages during times such as mid-consultation, or when watching a video, 
may also cause a user to favour the competition. 
 
The app itself should have minimal errors, and most errors should be caught and remediated via 
early and regular testing. 
3.2.5. Compatibility 
Our app should be fully functional on both Android, the 2 primary operating systems for mobile 
platforms.  
 
3.2.6. Other Requirement  
Documentation Requirements: Comprehensive user and technical documentation shall be 
maintained, including guides, system details, and version-controlled change logs to support 
effective usage, development, and maintenance. 
Development Standard Requirements: Our application shall be developed following industry 
best practices and platform coding standards, with continuous testing and compliance to UI/UX 
and accessibility guidelines to ensure reliability, quality, and user inclusivity. 
Security Requirements: All sensitive data shall be securely encrypted in transit and at rest, 
with strong authentication, authorization, and error monitoring mechanisms to prevent 
unauthorized access and detect security threats. 
Regulatory and Compliance Requirements: Our app shall comply with Singapore’s PDPA by 
ensuring responsible data handling, obtaining users’ informed consent, and requiring all

Maintenance Requirements: Our system shall be regularly updated and maintained for

4. Development Tools & Infrastructure 
4.1. Frontend: React Native + Expo | TanStack Query 
When deciding upon a frontend framework, we decided that due to the time constraints of a 
student project, we would want something that prioritises velocity and rapid iteration. 
The programmers in our team were (are) not well-versed in mobile development, but have 
moderate experience with React on the frontend.  
Thus, React Native seemed like the natural choice - being syntactically very similar. 
 
Expo is a popular framework that provides an SDK layered over React Native that provides 
conveniences which help to greatly speed up the development process, such as: 
● Fast Refresh, no need to keep rebuilding APK/IPA. Instantly test app on your mobile 
device via Expo Go 
● SDK for easy platform-agnostic access to native API’s (camera, location, sensors...) 
● Built app is automatically compatible with Android - no need for extra configuration  
 
On the more technical side of things, we want to ensure that our codebase remains relatively 
maintainable as it scales, so as to not crumble under the weight of technical debt. Tanstack 
Query is a popular library that assists in managing the complexities of managing server-side 
data caching and synchronisation on the frontend, which integrates well with React by following 
its hook-based paradigm. 
101 

4.2. Backend: FastAPI | SQLAlchemy + Alembic | PyTest 
Similarly, after discussions with the programmers - we decided upon Python for its suitability for 
rapid development, rich ecosystem (in case we also want to integrate AI libraries into our app), 
as well as ease of onboarding. 
After some research, we settled upon using FastAPI. Though it is relatively new (compared to 
more mature and well-known frameworks such as Django and Flask), it provides many modern 
features and conveniences, such as:  
● First-class Async support  
● Runtime data validation and strong type enforcement for request/response body  
● Built-in autogenerated Swagger UI documents. Easy for multiple backend developers to 
collaborate 
 
Coming from statically-typed languages, we were all too familiar with the importance of 
compile-time type safety, so we decided upon using SQLAlchemy as our Object-Relational 
Mapping (ORM) to ensure that we could declaratively define, and quickly iterate upon, our 
database schema, with Alembic to handle migrations. 
 
And finally, our product will make use of extensive tests, with unit testing being one of them. For 
that, we just picked what seemed to be the most popular unit testing framework currently, 
PyTest. 
4.3. Database: PostgreSQL 
In-spite of the rise in popularity of NoSQL/non-relational technologies in recent years, seemingly 
enticing due to their offer of flexibility - there is a tradeoff in terms of things like Data Integrity, 
Schema Enforcement, and Complex Queries/Joins. 
By using relational databases, we ensure that any data entering our system adheres to a strict 
pre-defined schema, reducing the likelihood of malformed data entering the system - something 
a NoSQL database like MongoDB is highly prone to. 
 
Relational databases are also much more mature, having been around for much longer than 
non-relational technologies, and have a proven track record of reliability. 
With most of us having previously taken Database Systems, which made use of PostgreSQL - 
we decided upon to use it due to our familiarity with it as a database technology. 
4.4. Version-Control: GitHub (CI/CD) 
For version control, we have chosen to make use of GitHub, simply for its ubiquity within the 
development space, its generous free tier (more than sufficient for a student project), and the 
built-in solution for CI/CD via Github Actions. 
Though enterprise environments may prefer to self-host on-premise repositories using 
something like GitLab or BitBucket, it would incur additional overhead for which their extra 
features do not justify. 
102 

Additionally, though GitHub Actions is a relatively new feature - being released in late 2019, it is 
sufficient for the (very) large majority of use-cases in building a robust CI/CD pipeline.  
4.5. DevOps/Infra: AWS | Docker  
 
Amazon Web Services (AWS) is currently the leading cloud provider, offering a vast suite of 
cloud services that operate upon a global infrastructure. 
To mitigate the complexity of self-hosting and managing infrastructure, we will leverage AWS’s 
suite of cloud services, with their promise of high availability and durability. 
AWS EC2: Elastic Compute Cloud, a horizontally-scalable service suitable for hosting backends  
AWS RDS: A managed database service, compatible with a wide range of database engines 
(including PostgreSQL)    
AWS S3: An object-storage service,  
We will also be using Docker as our containerisation technology. The reason is two-fold: 
● “It works on my machine”: By containerising the backend, we package all its 
dependencies into a single Docker image that is runnable by any machine with the 
Docker software, ensuring consistency - the code and local environment are the same 
as what is being deployed
 
● Local “test” Database: A dockerized PostgreSQL instance allows developers to quickly 
set-up and tear-down local databases, which is critical for rapid prototyping and iteration 
(a single CLI command, over having to manually configure database software)

4.7. Development Methodology  
After some consideration, our team has decided not to adhere to any formal project 
development methodology such as Scrum, Waterfall, or Kanban. 
However, we will still be making use of the Kanban Board for general task tracking. 
104 

Waterfall 
 
The Waterfall model is often regarded as the most rigid and linear of the traditional software 
development methodologies.  
The development lifecycle is conceptualized as a series of sequential phases, where each 
phase is usually well-defined, has well-understood requirements, and has little/no overlap with 
the other phases. 
However, this is inherently inflexible and has been known to present challenges when trying to 
accommodate changing requirements. Thus, our team has decided against it. 
Scrum 
 
Several of our team members already possess prior experience working with Scrum, allowing us 
to make the informed decision NOT to proceed with adopting this framework. 
Despite its popularity and widespread appeal in agile environments, Scrum would introduce 
considerable overhead via its prescribed ceremonies (daily standup, sprint planning, 
retrospectives, etc....), demanding time that could have been better spent on documentation, or 
development of our product. 
105 

Kanban 
 
Arguably the most flexible of all the aforementioned methodologies. It is a very lightweight 
framework, highly-adaptable, and most importantly - does not impose rigid roles and 
ceremonies. 
They focus on a much more continuous workflow, with the Kanban board mirroring that by 
showing tasks flowing through the board as it progresses from conception to completion. 
Conclusion 
Our goal is to minimise any process or ceremonial overhead, thereby maximising time that could 
be spent on development or working on our documentation - hence our deliberate decision to 
refrain from committing to any formal development methodology or framework. 
 
106 

However, the Kanban Board still offers great value as a task management tool, allowing us to 
easily keep track of tasks in a visual way. With it, the general state of our progress can quickly 
be ascertained via a quick glance at the board.  
It is fully transparent to all parties - allowing us to quickly spot task statuses, workload 
distribution, and any potential bottlenecks.

6.8 CI/CD Implementation and Developer Experience  
6.8.1 Continuous Integration (CI) 
Our CI process is handled entirely through GitHub Actions and focuses on ensuring code quality 
and functionality with every commit. 
Integration Testing 
Tooling 
Python's 
pytest
 framework. 
Process A GitHub Actions workflow is triggered upon a pull request to the 
main 
branch 
Scope Integration tests are run against the FastAPI backend, specifically 
targeting the core HTTPS endpoints to validate expected API 
behavior. 
Local Emulation To facilitate local testing within the GitHub Actions runner, services 
like LocalStack were employed to emulate AWS S3 functionality, 
while a local Dockerized PostgreSQL instance was used for 
database interactions. This ensures the tests are representative of 
the production environment's data dependencies. 
6.8.2 Continuous Delivery (CD) 
The CD pipeline automates the packaging and deployment of the backend service to our 
production environment hosted on AWS. 
204 

Deployment to Infra 
1. Image Build: Upon successful completion of CI tests, a dedicated CD GitHub Actions 
workflow is initiated. This workflow builds the production-ready Docker image for the 
FastAPI backend 
2. Image Push: The built image is securely pushed to the Amazon Elastic Container 
Registry (ECR) 
3. Remote Deployment: The workflow then uses AWS Systems Manager (SSM) to send 
SSH commands to the target EC2 instance 
4. Application Update: The EC2 instance, which is configured to run the application, 
receives the command to pull the latest image from ECR and restart the containerized 
application. 
Infrastructure Environment 
The deployed backend runs on an AWS EC2 instance and interacts with core AWS services: 
 
● Database: Amazon RDS PostgreSQL instance 
● Object Storage: Amazon S3 Bucket for persistent storage of images 
Developer Experience  
The implemented CI/CD practices and local development setup have significantly streamlined 
the development process: 
Streamlined Development Environment 
A robust local development environment ensures developers can work efficiently without relying 
on the shared cloud infrastructure for daily coding and testing.
